# Split-Step Trainer

This app helps you train the timing of your split-step and subsequent movement in pickleball. It shows animated feet reacting to a simulated paddle strike, so you can practice footwork patterns synchronized to your opponent hitting the ball.

## How a rep works

  1. A large circle shrinking to a point represents the ball traveling to and making contact with your opponent's paddle.
  2. Just before impact, take a small hop and land in the "split-step stance" with your feet separated and facing forards.
  3. Time your hop to land just as the circle shrinks to a point.
  4. A fraction of a second later, the on-screen feet move to show you how you need to react to this ball.
  5. After a brief pause, the drill resets and repeats automatically. An optional counter and timer track the length of your drill.

## Getting started

  1. Tap ⚙ to open settings.
  2. Under Reaction Movements, check the movements you want to practice. Uncheck all the movements to just practice the split-step hop.
  3. Adjust Min/Max Wait to control how long between reps (longer = more realistic match rhythm; shorter = higher-volume training).
  4. Tap Start Drill and place your device in front of you.
  5. Tap anywhere on screen to end the drill.

## Customizing movements

Tap Edit Movements to open the movement editor. Drag a foot's heel to reposition it, or drag its toe to rotate it. Enable Mirror Feet for a stance where the feet are mirrored, so positioning one foot automatically positions the other one.
